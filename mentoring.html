Georgia Tech

Jan. 2019 — Nov. 2019

The super-resolution fluorescent microscopy techniques have greatly enlightened neural anatomical and functional studies at the molecular level in recent years. Not only sub-neuronal structures, but also inter-neuronal skeleton and synaptic organization have been observed in a way that has never been done before. Moreover, light field microscopy, which allows simultaneous capture of both spatial and angular information, is a suitable tool for live imaging of various biological systems. A focused optical design for the original light field microscopy provides high-resolution images for fine sub-cellular structures.

However, simultaneous anatomical and functional imaging for inter-neuronal morphology and interactions have not been synchronized and is remaining highly demanded. The objective of the project is to develop a new integrated imaging system combining super-resolution and light-field microscopy for structure-function mapping with an ultra-high resolution in both the spatial and temporal domain.

In addition to the proposed technology, the project also aims to extensively train the Mentee with advanced microscopy instrumentation, bio-photonic and neurobiological knowledge, computer programming skills, scientific presentation and writing skills.
